How to play Colony

You run an ant colony competing with two rival colonies for the same ground and food. Each cycle (turn) you get 3 Action Points. Spend them, then tap Advance Cycle to resolve the turn.

  • Pulsing green tiles are empty ground next to one of your chambers — tap one to dig a Storage Chamber, Fungus Farm, or Nursery.
  • Pulsing blue tiles are resource nodes you've discovered but haven't connected — tap "Auto-Route" for the fastest path from your nest, or "Draw Path Manually" to tap out the route tile-by-tile yourself (useful for routing around a rival's territory).
  • Trails take a few cycles to deliver resources, and need spare foragers to run (see the Colony panel below the map to reassign Workers ↔ Foragers ↔ Soldiers).
  • Trails crossing a rival's territory turn yellow (contested). An undefended contested trail can leak resources to raiders — but if you garrison it with soldiers, a raid instead triggers a tactical battle you play out directly: defend your forager and fight off the raiders. Rivals defend their own garrisoned trails too, and can raid yours or each other's.
  • Fungus Farms turn sugar into fungus — soldiers and scouts eat fungus, workers and foragers eat sugar, so a standing defense costs upkeep.
  • Hitting certain milestones (total resources harvested, battles won, chambers built, sustained long trails) permanently unlocks colony-wide bonuses.
  • No fixed win condition — the goal is to keep your colony growing and your supply lines safe.

Map icons: gold ant = nest, tan crate = storage, purple mushroom = fungus farm, cream egg = nursery, pink crystal = sugar, red morsel = protein, blue gem = mineral.

Tile borders: blue = you, red and purple = the two rivals, yellow = contested.

Tips

  • Secure a sugar trail first, before anything richer-looking. Sugar feeds every ant — protein and mineral are useless to a starving colony. If sugar ever dips low, fix that before expanding further.
  • Don't overgrow your economy. Population only grows when you have a healthy sugar buffer, but a colony can still out-grow its income if you rush protein-fueled growth — watch your sugar trend, not just the current number.
  • A trail's capacity needs foragers to match. Upgrading capacity or laying new trails does nothing if you don't have spare foragers to run them — check the Colony panel before committing.
  • Garrison your most valuable contested trail, not all of them. Garrisoning costs soldiers you could otherwise use elsewhere, and soldiers eat fungus — defend what actually matters.
  • Losing a battle only costs that shipment and the units in it — a lost battle isn't a lost game. Retreating your forager (hold position away from raiders) can save the ant even if the cargo is lost.
  • Manual path drawing is for routing around danger, not efficiency — a longer path around a rival's territory avoids contested-trail leaks, at the cost of slower delivery (latency scales with path length).
  • Build a Fungus Farm before training soldiers — soldiers desert quickly without fungus income, and a deserted soldier's garrison slot doesn't come back on its own.
